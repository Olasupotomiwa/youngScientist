Level of confidence, level of significant and critical value relationship.

last lesson we learned different terms used in estimation. Here we are going to be taking a look at how they are related and the fomular connecting them

For Level of significant and confidence level
level of significant=1-confidence level α=1−CL (provided that CL is in decimal form)
OR
α=100-CL (if Confidence Level is to be left in percentage)
For level of significant and Critical value
to get the critical value we looks in to our statistical table. it could be in the form of any of the following
$Z_{\alpha}$ (one tail and large or normal distribution data)
$Z_{\frac{\alpha}{2}}$(two tail and large data or normally distributed)

$t_{\alpha}$(one tail and small data or not normally distributed)

$t_{\frac{\alpha}{2}}$(two tail and small data or not normally distributed)

Note: Z or t is used to denote the critical value it all depends on the type of data and the size of the sample given. A sample is said to be large if its size is greater than or equal to 30 i.e when $n \ge30$.

Table1 above can be easily done as soon as we know the first column value.
column2 is gotten by subtracting column1 from 100. e.g (100-90),(100-95),(100-97.5) etc.(confidence level and alpha level is added up to 100 all the time.)
Column3 is calculated by dividing column2 by 2 e.g (10/2),(5/2),(2.5/2) etc.
Column4 is gotten from the statistic table by checking respective value of column2 from Normal Distribution table. You can click Here to learn how to use the table.
Column5 is gotten just like column4 from the table only that we check for the respective value of column3 from the normal distribution table.

Note: we usually used column4 when we are doing one-tailed test while column5 is for two-tailed test. when you are dealing with a question that needed critical value as soon as the tyype of test to be used is not given in any form you are expected to used two-tails.
