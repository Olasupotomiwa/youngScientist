Margin of Error,Standard Error and Standard Deviation Relationship.

To calculate the standard deviation from any given data should not be new to you. However, shouldn't we take a look at some other important formula?? The following formula shows the relationship

$S.E=\frac{S}{\sqrt n}$ or $S.E=\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt n}$

$M.E=S.E Z_{\frac{\alpha}{2}}$ or $M.E=S.E Z_{\alpha}$

$M.E=Z\frac{S}{\sqrt n}$ or $M.E= Z\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt n}$

$M.E= t{n-1}*\frac s{\sqrt n}$ or $M.E= t_{(n-1)}*\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt n}$

S and $\sigma$ represent the sample and population standarddeviation respectively.

S.E and M.E denote Standard Error and Margin of Error respectively.

n means the sample size.

Z and $t_{n-1}$ represent the critical value gotten from the normal distribution table and t-table respectively.

what are the relationship between the parameters that make up the formula??

Note that whenever you have a formula any variable at the numerator will have positive relationship with the subject of the formula while denominators has negative relashionship. Hence this leads us to the following fact.

1. looking at S.E formula increase in standard deviation(numerator) causes increase in standard error, decrease in standard deviation causes decrease in standard error(standard deviation has positive relationship with S.E)

2.Increase sample size(denominator) causes decrease in standard error and decrease in sample size increase the standard error(sample size has negative relationship with S.E)

3. looking at the M.E formular. M.E has positive relationship with S.E, so ME increases when S.E increases.

4. M.E increases when standard deviation increases (since S.E will increase according to no1)

5. M.E decreases when sample size increases, M.E increases when sample size decreases.
Now let's put the formula in to practice.

Standard Error of a mean(1 poputlation)

Here you are dealing with one population and standard deviation can be found or already given. the below formula should be used
$ \sigma_x =\cfrac {\sigma}{\sqrt n}$

Example1

In a certain cement company, workers have a mean hourly wage of #1200 with a population standard deviation of #30. Given a sample size of 36, estimate and interpret the SE of the sample mean:

Solution

$ \sigma_x =\cfrac {\sigma}{\sqrt n}=\cfrac {30}{\sqrt {36}}$
30/6= #5

Interpretation: if we were to draw several samples of size 36 from the employee population and construct a sampling distribution of the sample means, we would end up with a mean of #1200 and a standard error of #5.
Note: $ \sigma_x$ usually denoted stanadard error sometimes.

Example 2

Assuming the size in example2 has been increased to 64. what is the standard error?

Solution

$\sigma_x =\cfrac {\sigma}{\sqrt n}$
$\frac {30}{\sqrt {64}}$
$30/8= # 3.75 $

Example 3

calculate the standard error of the mean given the following sample. 22,33,33,30,25,53,56,32,28,27.

Solution

we know that $ S.E =\cfrac {\sigma}{\sqrt n}$ Since we are given only the data here, therefore we need to calculate the standard deviation by ourselves.

Now that we know the standard deviation we can now calculate the standard error.

$ \sigma_x =\cfrac {\sigma}{\sqrt n}$$ $$\cfrac {11.4353}{\sqrt {10}}$
$11.4353/3.162= 3.616477$
Hence the standard error of the mean is 3.616477.

Standard Error of a Proportion(A poputlation)

sometimes you might not be dealing with mean or standard deviation, our data might be in form of percentage or proportion, in this case the following formula is used for standard error.

$S.E=\sqrt{\frac{P(1-P)}{n}}$
$OR$
$S.E=\sqrt{\frac{p(q)}{n}}$
where P represent the proportion given. q denote (1-P)

Example 1

In the last revision class, 75% actively participated. What is the standard error if the population of students in attendance was 2500?

Solution

P=75% =0.75 (alwas convert to decimal) n=2500 (total number)
$S.E=\sqrt{\frac{0.75(1-0.75)}{n}}$

$S.E=\sqrt{\frac{0.75(0.25)}{2500}}$

$S.E=\sqrt{\frac{0.1875}{2500}}$

$S.E=\sqrt{0.000075}$

$ S.E=0.008660254$

Example 2

Out of 1500 students offering sta114 onl 250 of them failed the course what is the standard error of the proportion of those who failed.

Solution

Here we are given the numbers hence we need to find the proportion first.
P=no of students failed/total number of student
P=250/1500 =0.1667 (alwas convert to decimal)
$n=1500 (totalNumber)$
$S.E=\sqrt{\frac{0.1667(1-0.166)}{n}}$

$S.E=\sqrt{\frac{0.1667(0.8333)}{1500}}$

$S.E=\sqrt{\frac{0.1389111}{1500}}$

$S.E=\sqrt{0.0000926}$

$ S.E=0.009623274$
