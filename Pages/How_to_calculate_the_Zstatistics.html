I believe you should already know how to calculate the Zstatistics but should you don’t I am going to teach how to do that here. well, You don't need much but you must know the following :

  1. the question can either comes in form of a population or two different population

  2. The target parameter will either be mean or proportion

  3. Be careful when you are dealing with two population( either the mean or proportion)

The most important formula needed for calculating the Zstatistics are of four category.

  1. Using a sample mean, sample size and standard Deviation

  2. Using the a proportion and sample size

  3. Using two sample mean, size and standard deviation from two different population

  4. Using two proportions and sample size from two different population

Note: the population mean or proportion is always given

How to Calculate the Zstatistics of a population

Here we will learn how to calculate Zstatistics when dealing with hust a population. This is of two category

Sample mean, standard deviation and sample size are given.

$$Z_{cal}=\frac{\bar X-μ}{σ/\sqrt{n}}$$ $$\bar X = sampleMean$$ $$σ = Standard Deviation$$ $$ n = sample size$$

It should be noted that the denominator can be replace by Standard Error. since $$S.E=\frac{σ}{\sqrt{n}}$$

when proportion and sample size are given

$$Z_{cal}=\frac{P-P_0}{\sqrt{\frac{P_0(1-P_0)}{n}}}$$ $$ P_0 = population Proportion$$ $$P = SampleProportion$$

It should be noted that the denominator can be replace by Standard Error. since $$S.E=\sqrt{\frac{P_0(1-P_0)}{n}}$$

How to Calculate the Zstatistics of Two population

Here we will learn how to calculate Zstatistics when dealing with hust a population. This is of two category

Formula for calculating Zstatistics when Sample mean, size and standard deviation from two different population is given.

$$Z_{cal}=\frac{x_1-x_2}{\sqrt{{\frac{σ_1}{n_1}}+{\frac{σ_2}{n_2}}}}$$ $ x_1 =$ sample mean of the first population
$ x_2 =$ Sample mean of the second population
$ \sigma_1 =$ standard deviation of the first population
$ \sigma_2 =$ standard deviation of the second population
$ n_1 =$ sample size of the first population
$ n_2 =$ Sample size of the second population

it is use to test for significant differences of two population mean

Formula for calculating Zstatistics of two population given the proportions and sample size

$$P_0=\frac{n_1 P_1 + n_2P_2}{n_1+n_2}$$ $$Z_{cal}=\frac{P_1-P_2}{\sqrt{P_0(1-P_0)(\frac{1}{n_1}+\frac{1}{n_2})}}$$ $P_1$ is the proportion of the first sample
$P_2$ is the proportion of the second sample
$n_1$ is the size of the first sample from the first population
$n_2$ is the size of the second sample from the second population.
$(P_1-P_2)$ is the difference between the two proportions.

this is use to check for the significant differences of proportion between two population
